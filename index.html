Early October · 5–7 days

Trekking Italy

Six hand-picked routes — from the Dolomites and the Ligurian coast to Tuscany, the Amalfi cliffs and wild Sardinia. Pick the one that fits the group, and let's lock dates.

🍂 Best window: 1–15 October · golden light, empty trails

Why October?

Early October is a sweet spot across Italy: golden larches and crisp light in the Dolomites, warm sun and harvest season on the coast and in Tuscany, and blissfully quiet trails everywhere after the summer crowds. The one catch is altitude — high mountain huts (rifugi) start closing in late September, so the routes below are all built around sleeping in towns and hotels rather than huts.

Planning note. The classic Dolomites hut-to-hut traverse (Alta Via 1) was dropped from this shortlist — most of its high rifugi close by late September, so October is poor timing. All six options below are town- or hotel-based (you sleep in real beds, not huts), which makes them far more reliable for our dates. The southern and coastal options — Cinque Terre, Amalfi and Sardinia — are the warmest and most October-proof of all.

① Tre Cime / Sesto Dolomites — Iconic Peaks

Valley base: Dobbiaco or Sesto · day hikes

Why: Maximum iconic scenery, minimum logistical risk. You sleep in a valley hotel and day-hike. The Tre Cime di Lavaredo — three vertical towers — are the most photographed peaks in the Alps, and October's golden light + empty trails make it magical.

② Alpe di Siusi / Seiser Alm — Gentle Golden Meadows

Valley base: Castelrotto or Compatsch · day hikes

Why: Europe's largest high-altitude meadow — rolling golden pastures under the Sassolungo and Sciliar peaks. The easiest terrain and smoothest logistics of all three. Big views without big climbs; perfect for a mixed-ability group and a relaxed pace.

Difficulty: Easy–Moderate 7–14 km/day 2–5 h walking Daypack only Easiest logistics

③ Cinque Terre + Portofino — The Ligurian Coast

Clifftop coastal trails · Levanto → Portovenere · point-to-point

Why: A warm, sea-level alternative for when the mountains turn cold. Cliff-top paths link five pastel fishing villages, with the Portofino promontory as a stunning add-on. October is arguably the best month: mild temperatures, harvest season (grapes, pesto, focaccia), swimmable-ish sea, and a fraction of the summer crowds. The most weather-proof and scenic of the warm options.

ℹ️ Note: Some low coastal links (e.g. the Via dell'Amore) open/close depending on landslide repairs — the higher trails used above are always open. Train connects all five villages, so days are easy to shorten or skip.

④ Via Francigena — Lucca → Siena (Tuscany)

Town-to-town pilgrim walk · point-to-point · ~135 km

Why: A completely different flavour from the Dolomites — a historic pilgrimage route through the heart of Tuscany. Rolling cypress hills, vineyards, gravel farm roads and medieval hilltop towns. You sleep in a different town every night (B&Bs / pilgrim lodgings), so there are no huts to worry about. It's a walk-and-wine-and-culture trip more than an alpine trek — and it's the most weather-proof option for early October (grape & olive harvest season).

ℹ️ Note: This is the route from a friend's suggestion. It's not in the Dolomites — it's central Tuscany — so it offers a genuinely different style: cultural / gastronomic walking rather than mountain scenery. A couple of stages are long (29–30 km) on gravel/road.

⑤ Path of the Gods — Amalfi Coast

Sorrento peninsula + Amalfi · day hikes from a coastal base

Why: Southern Italy's most famous trail — the Sentiero degli Dei, a clifftop balcony path soaring high above the sea between Agerola and Positano, with lemon groves, Capri on the horizon and pastel villages tumbling to the water. Warmer and more dramatic than Cinque Terre, and October is ideal: summer heat and crowds gone, sea still mild.

ℹ️ Note: Furthest south — more travel to get there (fly to Naples). Lots of stone staircases; can be busy near Positano/Capri even in October, but trails thin out quickly. Easy to base in one town (Sorrento or Amalfi) and use buses/ferries.

⑥ Selvaggio Blu — Sardinia

Golfo di Orosei · wild coast traverse · point-to-point

Why: Italy's wildest and most legendary trek — limestone cliffs plunging into a turquoise sea, hidden coves reachable only on foot or by boat, no roads, no villages. A true adventure. October is the perfect window: the fierce summer heat is gone and the sea is still swimmable.

A gentler "cove-to-cove" day-hike version (with boat transfers between beaches) exists if the group isn't technical. Fly to Cagliari or Olbia.

My steer for early October. For mountain scenery, go with ① Tre Cime / Sesto for jaw-dropping peaks, or ② Alpe di Siusi for a relaxed pace. For the warmest, most reliable weather, the coast wins: ③ Cinque Terre + Portofino is the easy crowd-pleaser, while ⑤ Path of the Gods (Amalfi) is the more dramatic, iconic southern option. Pick ④ Via Francigena for a cultural pilgrim walk through Tuscany, or ⑥ Selvaggio Blu if the group is fit and wants a genuine wild adventure. (The classic Alta Via 1 traverse was dropped — October is too late for its huts.)
