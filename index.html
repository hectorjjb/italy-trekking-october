🥾 Italy Trekking — Early October

Four hand-picked routes · Dolomites · Liguria coast · Tuscany · 5–7 days

Choose your adventure: iconic peaks, gentle meadows, a clifftop coast, or a Tuscan pilgrim walk

Best window: 1–15 October · golden larches, empty trails, crisp light

Why October?

Early October in the Dolomites means golden larch forests, dramatic low-angle light on the pale limestone, and trails that are blissfully quiet after the summer crowds. The trade-off: most high mountain huts (rifugi) close between late September and early October, and the first snow can dust the high passes.

⚠️ Planning note: The classic Dolomites hut-to-hut traverse (Alta Via 1) was dropped from this shortlist — most of its high rifugi close by late September, so October is poor timing. All four options below are town- or hotel-based (you sleep in real beds, not huts), which makes them far more reliable for our October dates. Options ③ & ④ (the coast and Tuscany) are lower, warmer, and the most October-proof of all.

① Tre Cime / Sesto Dolomites — Iconic Peaks

Valley base: Dobbiaco or Sesto · day hikes

Why: Maximum iconic scenery, minimum logistical risk. You sleep in a valley hotel and day-hike. The Tre Cime di Lavaredo — three vertical towers — are the most photographed peaks in the Alps, and October's golden light + empty trails make it magical.

② Alpe di Siusi / Seiser Alm — Gentle Golden Meadows

Valley base: Castelrotto or Compatsch · day hikes

Why: Europe's largest high-altitude meadow — rolling golden pastures under the Sassolungo and Sciliar peaks. The easiest terrain and smoothest logistics of all three. Big views without big climbs; perfect for a mixed-ability group and a relaxed pace.

Difficulty: Easy–Moderate 7–14 km/day 2–5 h walking Daypack only ✅ Easiest logistics

③ Cinque Terre + Portofino — The Ligurian Coast

Clifftop coastal trails · Levanto → Portovenere · point-to-point

Why: A warm, sea-level alternative for when the mountains turn cold. Cliff-top paths link five pastel fishing villages, with the Portofino promontory as a stunning add-on. October is arguably the best month: mild temperatures, harvest season (grapes, pesto, focaccia), swimmable-ish sea, and a fraction of the summer crowds. The most weather-proof and scenic of the warm options.

ℹ️ Note: Some low coastal links (e.g. the Via dell'Amore) open/close depending on landslide repairs — the higher trails used above are always open. Train connects all five villages, so days are easy to shorten or skip.

④ Via Francigena — Lucca → Siena (Tuscany)

Town-to-town pilgrim walk · point-to-point · ~135 km

Why: A completely different flavour from the Dolomites — a historic pilgrimage route through the heart of Tuscany. Rolling cypress hills, vineyards, gravel farm roads and medieval hilltop towns. You sleep in a different town every night (B&Bs / pilgrim lodgings), so there are no huts to worry about. It's a walk-and-wine-and-culture trip more than an alpine trek — and it's the most weather-proof option for early October (grape & olive harvest season).

ℹ️ Note: This is the route from a friend's suggestion. It's not in the Dolomites — it's central Tuscany — so it offers a genuinely different style: cultural / gastronomic walking rather than mountain scenery. A couple of stages are long (29–30 km) on gravel/road.

🏆 My steer for early October: For mountain scenery, go with ① Tre Cime / Sesto for jaw-dropping peaks with low hassle, or ② Alpe di Siusi if we want it relaxed. For the warmest, most reliable weather with sea views and great food, the ③ Cinque Terre + Portofino coast is my top pick — even more weather-proof than the mountains. Choose ④ Via Francigena if the group specifically wants the pilgrim-walk / medieval-town experience. (The classic Alta Via 1 traverse was dropped — October is too late for its huts.)
